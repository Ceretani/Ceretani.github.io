About me

I am a mathematician working on PDEs and associated optimization and control problems. I received my Ph.D. in Mathematics (2016) and undergraduate degrees in Mathematics — Licenciatura en Matemática (Sciences, 2010) and Profesorado en Matemática (Education, 2007) — all from Universidad Nacional de Rosario, Argentina. My research combines analytical tools with applications to fluid dynamics, heat transfer and environmental modeling. I have held positions in Argentina and Germany, and conducted research visits in Italy, the USA and Ecuador. I am currently assistant researcher at CONICET (Argentina) and affiliate faculty at George Mason University (USA).

Projects, outreach & service

Applied project: RAD.AR (Radón Argentina)

An interdisciplinary initiative bringing together researchers from applied mathematics, geology and health sciences to address indoor radon exposure — the second leading cause of lung cancer worldwide. The team conducted the first systematic indoor radon survey in Argentina (Punilla Valley, Córdoba), identifying geological controls and seasonal variations, and developed mathematical models to evaluate ventilation strategies for reducing radon concentration in homes. Preliminary results have been published in Environmental Earth Sciences (2025) and presented at MACI 2025, as well as in scientific meetings (PROSA 2023, SSAGI 2024). Ongoing field campaigns and further development of mathematical models for mitigation strategies continue to advance the project. More information: @radonargentina on Instagram.
